The Pod
September - December 2018

Collaborators | Alia Albastaki and Ying Wang

The Pod externalizes and amplifies the human body. It answers the idea of a body extension as an extension of the physical self by speculating the facilitation of mental and metaphysical extension. Visual and physical stimuli increase the user’s awareness of the body as flesh, provoking the examination of their self-perception. Sensory engagement encourages introspection by presenting the individual with their self via amplification of normally suppressed or unobserved sensory experiences.

Experience

Users participate freely, opening, entering, and closing the lids over them. Once inside, the user’s arm and leg movements are restricted by the structure, as is the visibility of their body. Only able to see their face reflected in a mirror, users must rely on tactile sensation to know the position of their body. Dim mood lighting projected sequentially around 360 degrees distorts the reflected face, disrupting users’ normal perception of themselves. This disruption facilitates reflection on the multiple facets of their identity. Organic sounds of the inner organs, including the breath, the heartbeat, and those of the abdominopelvic cavity, vibrate the structure and the user. From the outside, these sounds challenge the perception of machines as cold and lifeless. For the user, whole-body vibration of internal bodily sounds solidifies the link between the physical self and the Pod.

The physical restrictions and audiovisuals facilitate the elimination of the boundary between user and Pod. The resulting human-machine entity disconnects from the outside world and embarks on an immersive experience that blurs the boundaries between extension and reflection.

Contribution

The entire team contributed to each stage of development, from ideation, through rapid prototyping, to the installation of the Pod. My main contribution was leading the design, manufacture, and assembly of the structural components of all to-scale prototypes, as well as the final wood and metal structure. I also assisted with the functional testing and installation of audiovisual components.

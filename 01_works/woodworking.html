About cv
Woodworking

As an assistant in the NYUAD Wood Shop, much of my time is spent supporting the wood shop instructor with student, faculty, and institutional projects. After hours, I often stay or return to the shop to work on my own independent pieces under the supervision of the instructor. The pieces shown here end up as gifts to friends and family. Needless to say, both the informal and formal aspects of the job have been invaluable to my development as a designer. Grappling with the physical world and making myself useful to a craftsman has taught me to:

  • anticipate and satisfy the wants and needs of others by extrapolating from observed behavior and processes.
  • stay present, mindful, and intentional when working on challenging projects and in potentially high risk environments.
  • anticipate fabrication problems and appropriately estimate the time cost of a project.
  • ask better questions and seek to understand before seeking to explain.

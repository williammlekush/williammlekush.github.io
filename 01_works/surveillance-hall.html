Surveillance Hall
Novemeber - December 2019

Full Documentation

At the end of the fall semester in 2019, I designed and built a performative installation piece for the NYUAD Interactive Media exhibition.

Experience

This hall makes use of the movements of the human body to disrupt the exploitation of human data through surveillance. As users move through the hallway, the hall translates their spatial data into an audiovisual experience. While the audio modulation provides feedback to the user that their movements through the space cause change, the visuals display outside of the hallway to the public. Hence, instead of an anonymous group in a private space observing the public, the public observes users interacting inside the hall. In addition, rather than using the surveillance data to exploit consumers and prosecute citizens, the data facilitates interactive, performative, and collaborative art.

Process

Special thanks to Lab Manager and Instructor Ume Hussein, Instructor Jack Du, Professor Michael Shiloh, Professor Aaron Sherwood, Mari Calderon, Adham Chakohi, Instructor Judi Olsen, and Professor Matthew Karau.

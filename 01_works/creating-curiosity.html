Creating Curiosity
September 2018

Science conferences provide an opportunity for scientists to come together, exchange ideas and research, and communicate with the public. Annual NYUAD Research Conferences in the past struggled with this final task, with a lack of visual accessibility and brand identity during the conferences. In preparation for the upcoming 2018 NYUAD Research Conference, the organizers hosted a poster competition to spur ideas and collaborations for the conferences visual identity. I worked with a small team of graphic designers to design and pitch the poster shown above.

Concept

Each visual element contributes to evoking a sense of curiosity. The eye draws people into the poster, creating an immediate personal connection that stands out among the plethora of posters hung on the walls around the university. Obscuring the face renders both gaze and identity of the individual ambiguous. The two hands reference the game of peek-a-boo, but using two different people creates an additional layer of questions. Who is obscuring the face? Why are they hiding the face of the other person? Who is the person? All of these questions drive the viewer to become curious about the poster, with the logo offering an the answer: come to the conference and find out.

Contribution

My contribution to this project included:

  • Facilitation of the ideation process through divergent thinking exercises.
  • Production of the working photograph, including organizing models and a photographer and managing the photo shoot.
  • Initial edits to the working photograph.

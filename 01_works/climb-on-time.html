Climb On Time
March - May 2020

Full Documentation

Concept

For centuries, clocks have kept the rhythm of human activity. In the current moment, clocks dominates our lives, telling us when and how long to wake, eat, work, meet, rest, exercise, play, and sleep. However, while modern life takes timekeeping for granted, clocks like Al-Jaziri's Elephant Clock represent cultural and scientific achievements in mechanical and systematic design.

In the spirit of these mechanical clocks, the NYUAD Machine Lab class of Spring 2020 decided to design, fabricate, and display a wall-sized clock in the NYUAD Arts Center. At different intervals throughout the day, different mechanisms activate to express a representation of the contributing artists derived from their hobbies. Due to the coronavirus pandemic, designs of the mechanism and overall structure wait to be fabricated when the fabrication shops open and the artists return.

Contribution

In contribution to this project, I designed a mechanism to mimic the basic movement of a rock climber. An elevator mechanism lifts a limb-movement mechanism up over the period of an hour. During this, the climber's opposing limbs alternate their movement: right leg and left arm up, then left leg and right arm, and so on. When the climber reaches the top at the end of the hour, the limb movement ceases, and the elevator mechanism releases quickly, causing the climber to fall to the bottom.
